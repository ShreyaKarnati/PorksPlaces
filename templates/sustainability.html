Sustainability Statement

It’s been a long 3 years since the pandemic went underway, but the WHO has, as of May 5th, declared its end. Whether this is a good decision or not, our lives have changed in many important ways. Did you know that the Pacific Northwest is a particularly beautiful place? Actually know, not just going off of faded memories. For many of us, we used to experience that beauty and tranquility firsthand, but it’s been so long since we have that we don’t even remember what we’ve forgotten.

This site is for filling in some of those gaps your memories have developed. The model is simple: we recommend places to go while giving you information about what it is, how far away is it, and a link to their website if you want more detail. You can view all the locations on a map on our maps page, or search for a specific Place by name or category on our search page. Then you decide if you want to go to those Places. Our good friend John Porker is there to ensure your decisions are wise.

Sustainability not only applies to the world around you, but to your life as well. Taking good care of your brain and body means there’s fewer obstacles to living your life how you want to. Just as protecting nature means that the world around you can continue to live and grow, existing in that nature, even for just a bit at a time, grounds you in that world. It can be scary to feel like you’re cut off from everything, having to face all your challenges alone. We hope that our site can help break that illusion. Not only by existing in nature, but as a social tool: as more people use this site, the higher the chances you’ll meet someone else in a Place.

Pork’s Places has just been born, but it will continue to grow. We plan to add new functionality such as showing bike trails and bus routes to show how to get to the Places in an environmentally conscious and cheap way.

We thank you for engaging with Pork's Places and hope we showed you some cool Places!
